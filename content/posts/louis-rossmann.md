[Louis Rossmann](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Rossmann) is an electronics technician, YouTuber, and consumer rights activist. He frequently posts videos to his [channel](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCl2mFZoRqjw_ELax4Yisf6w) about right-to-repair, data privacy, and copyright law. He has followed Flock usage and legislation in the United States.  

In early March, after the Virginia Crime Commission released its [report](https://vscc.virginia.gov/2026/VSCC%20ALPR%20Report%20January%202026%20FINAL%20REVISED%20Jan%2021%202026.pdf) on police departments' Flock usage, Louis posted a video explaining that the misuse of Flock data in violation of [Virginia law](https://lis.virginia.gov/bill-details/20251/HB2724/text/CHAP0720) is why "I oppose automated license plate readers in any way, shape, or form, even when you have laws in place that try to protect people from abuses that occur."
